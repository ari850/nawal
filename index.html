FAQ

Questions people ask first

Clear answers for everyday users and organizations considering a passwordless future.

What is NawalKey?

NawalKey is a passwordless identity system that replaces passwords entirely. Unlike a password manager, it does not store passwords in a vault. It verifies identity using trusted devices.

How do I log in?

Select “Log in with NawalKey,” unlock your trusted device with Face ID, fingerprint, or a PIN, and you are signed in instantly without entering a password.

Can I use multiple devices?

Yes. NawalKey works across phones, laptops, tablets, and desktops. New devices are approved by an existing trusted device before being added.

What if I lose a trusted device?

Your other devices continue to work. If it was your only device, secure recovery options such as a recovery code or IT verification can restore access.

Could someone unlock my accounts if they steal my device?

No. The device must still be unlocked using Face ID, fingerprint, or a PIN. Lost devices can also be revoked from another trusted device or a secure portal.

Do websites and apps need to support it?

NawalKey works best with websites and apps that support passkeys. For services still transitioning, it can operate alongside current login methods.

What if my employer uses a different system?

NawalKey is designed to work alongside existing identity systems such as Microsoft Entra, Google Workspace, and Okta through standard APIs.

Is my data private?

Yes. NawalKey does not store or see your passwords. Authentication happens locally on your device, and only a cryptographic proof is shared with the service.

Do I need to start over?

No. NawalKey works with existing accounts and allows gradual adoption as services move toward passwordless login.

Am I protected if a website gets hacked?

Yes. Because there are no stored passwords, there are no credentials for attackers to steal and reuse.
